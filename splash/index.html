Millburn Splash is an afternoon of mini, interactive classes for students in grades 1-8, taught by students at Millburn High School. The classes cover a wide variety of topics, from origami to entrepreneurship to robotics!


All proceeds from Millburn Splash support hackMHS. hackMHS is a student-organized event encouraging high school students to learn to code apps, games, and websites. To learn more about hackMHS, click here.

frequently asked questions

  • Who can register for classes?

    Our classes are designed for students in grades 1-8. Students in grades 9-12 should consider attending hackMHS for code and technology-related workshops!

  • Can parents accompany their children?

    Yes! Parents are encouraged to attend. There is no additional cost for parents attending, but due to classroom limitations, each student may only be accompanied by one parent.

  • How much does it cost to attend?

    Registration costs $15 per student and includes 5 sessions of classes, as well as snacks. Any additional donations will be much appreciated!

  • Where and when is the event?

    On Sunday, May 8, from 1-5pm at Millburn High School.

  • Who will be teaching the classes?

    Students at Millburn High School involved in organizing hackMHS will be teaching classes at Millburn Splash.

  • How do I get there?

    Millburn Splash takes place at 462 Millburn Avenue, Millburn, NJ. For Google Maps directions, click here.

  • I have a question not answered here.

    Feel free to email us at hackatmhs@gmail.com, and we'd be happy to answer your question!

Humanities


  • Beginner's American Sign Language

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Sofia Fernandez-Poyatos & Shannon Peng

    Learn the basics of American Sign Language, including signs for common phrases, objects, and the alphabet.

Arts

  • 3D Digital Modeling (Double Session)

    Grades: 5-8

    Instructors: Morgan Sun & Jackson Robbins

    Learn the basics of using a 3D modeling program, and create (and perhaps even 3D print) something of your own!

  • Digital Art & Graphic Design

    Grades: 3-8

    Instructors: Asena Yildiz

    Learn how to make art using technology. Digital art and graphic design is incredibly useful for many applications.

  • Songwriting

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Shannon Peng & Sofia Fernandez-Poyatos

    Explore basic techniques for songwriting and the different elements of popular songs—and then write your own!

  • Intro to Origami

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Anand Tyagi & Jackson Sanders

    Learn how to fold some basic origami animals and objects. No prior experience necessary!

  • Cartooning & Comic Book Drawing

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Asena Yildiz & Mohar Kalra

    Learn the basics of cartooning, from layout and story design to character design. The class will be divided into smaller groups by grade.

Business

  • Intro to Entrepreneurship & Pitching

    Grades: 6-8

    Instructors: Asena Yildiz

    Learn how to think creatively and outside the box to find innovative solutions to issues in our everyday lives, as well as how to present those ideas to others.

Electronics

  • Intro to Circuits

    Grades: 5-8

    Instructors: Morgan Sun

    Explore the basics behind circuits, and then experiment with building them: we will start with a basic LED circuit, and progress to analyzing an oscillator circuit.

  • Robotics in Today's World

    Grades: 4-8

    Instructors: Anand Tyagi & Mohar Kalra

    In this lecture, we will learn about the different robots that exist in today’s world, how we interact with robots everyday, and how the future will be affected by robotics. We will also touch upon how to get involved in robotics in the high school and further on.

  • Intro to Robotics: LEGO Mindstorms (Double Session)

    Grades: 3-8

    Instructors: Anand Tyagi & Morgan Sun

    Learn how to build and code a basic robot using LEGO Mindstorms kits.

  • Intro to Logic with Minecraft Redstone

    Grades: 4-8

    Instructors: David O'Sullivan

    An introduction to basic Redstone for those with little to no background, and introduction to logic gates. Students will be walked through the basics of redstone, how it relates to electrical concepts in real-life, and then will be taught logic gates and how they can be applied in both Minecraft and in real life. Students should have experience with basic controls in Minecraft.

Programming

  • Web Design with HTML

    Grades: 4-8

    Instructors: Shannon Peng & Abhinav Madahar

    Learn to code in HTML, the language of the web, and walk out with your own website! No prior experience necessary.

  • Learn to Code with Python (Double Session)

    Grades: 7-8

    Instructors: Abhinav Madahar & Raghav Chetal

    Python is a great first language to learn. In this class, you'll learn the key concepts behind programming, including variables, if-logic, and functions, and then apply your knowledge to create a text-based video game!

  • Learn to Code with Scratch

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: David O'Sullivan & Jackson Sanders

    A great introduction to programming for first time coders! Students will explore programming concepts through an easy to use drag-and-drop program and will get the opportunity to create their own fun projects while learning valuable code experience.

  • Video Games with Gamemaker

    Grades: 5-8

    Instructors: David O'Sullivan

    Students will learn basic concepts of video game design, and then get to apply these learned skills in an easy to use interface. Prior experience is not necessary.

Science

  • Mad Science: Chemistry

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Abhinav Madahar & Jeremy Cheng

    Explore chemistry demos and experiments, such as coke & mentos and non-newtonian fluids. Messy ingredients involved.

  • Mad Science: Physics

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Abhinav Madahar, Mohar Kalra, & Josh Broitman

    Explore chemistry demos and experiments, including magnetic slime, slinky drops, and more!

  • Engineering Challenge

    Grades: 1-5

    Instructors: Raghav Chetal, Asena Yildiz, & Mohar Kalra

    In this engineering challenge, students will be working in teams to create a simple structure using creativity and hands-on learning.

Miscellaneous

  • Chess

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: Josh Broitman & Shantanu Laghate

    Learn the rules and basic strategies behind the classic board game, chess.

  • Go

    Grades: 1-8

    Instructors: David O'Sullivan & Jackson Sanders

    Learn the ancient chinese abstract board game, “Go”. Students will learn the rules, and then delve into some basic strategy. They will then get the opportunity to play against others using their newfound skills.

  • Intro to Magic Tricks

    Grades: 4-8

    Instructors: Anand Tyagi

    In this course, we will learn basic magic tricks using coins and other household objects.
